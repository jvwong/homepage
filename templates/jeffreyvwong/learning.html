A First Course in Computer programming [with Python]


Why I'm In

Aimed at novices and beginners, this is meant to be an accessible introduction to computer programming. This course was motivated by encountering people who were:

  • Frustrated with the way technology was shaping their lives -- without their say
  • Curious about learning to do useful things with computers
  • Interested in getting ahead in their job or changing directions altogether


"Big Picture"

There's a lot of buzz around learning to "program" for the web. It is important to keep in mind that a great many people use programming for things that have nothing to do with the web. Just as most people who learn advanced math never become mathematicians, not everyone who learns programming has any intention of becoming a software engineer. I believe programming skills will be basic requirements in many professions in the future. Here we aim to use relevant projects to drive learning, sustain motivation, and ensure that programming remains relevant to the learner. Possible projects include:

  • Process, read, and write data from programs to text files
  • Automate reading and writing from spreadsheets
  • Perform rudimentary analytics on data scraped from the web


What's on Deck

At this stage, the course is in development and I am really interested in getting as much input as possible on what approaches would work best in a first course in learning to programming. Just as important, I want to hear opinions on what NOT to include. The course will aim to be:

  • Individually mentored and guided
    • Tailored to you the learner, not "one-size-fits-all"

  • Focused on the problem-solving nature of programming
    • Using projects to drive learning

  • Grounded in fundamentals
    • Not (another) course in front-end web development

Fill out the questionnaire and have your say!
